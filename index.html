Draft Grades

Every pick ranked against the points it actually scored that season. A late pick that outscores early picks is a steal; an early pick that busts is, well, a bust.

How This Is Measured

Pick Value = Draft Pick Number − Points Rank (where that player finished in total points that season, skill positions only). Pick him 50th, he finishes 10th → Value = +40, a steal. Pick him 1st, he finishes 100th → Value = −99, a bust.

A manager's Draft Class Value is the sum of every pick's value that season. Avg Value/Pick averages that across all their drafts, rewarding consistency over one lucky year. Best/Worst Draft is their single best/worst season. Steals/Busts are individual picks. Best & Worst Draft Classes Ever ranks single-season totals across everyone.

All-Time Draft Rankings

Ranked by average value per pick (pick number minus that season's points rank, averaged across every draft). Positive = consistently finds value; negative = consistently reaches.

Free Agent Grades

Every waiver/free-agent pickup graded by the points that player scored while actually on your roster.

How This Is Measured

Each pickup's value is that player's season-total points, prorated by the fraction of the season you actually held them (pickup week through the next time you dropped them, or season end). This is an approximation, not a week-by-week total — full weekly tracking wasn't practical to fetch for every transaction across 24 seasons, so a full season's production is scaled down to the portion of the season you rostered them. Avg Value/Season rewards managers who found value on the wire consistently, not just in one big year.

Trade Grades

Every trade compared side-by-side: points scored by what each manager received vs. what they gave up.

How This Is Measured

Same prorated-points approach as Free Agent Grades, applied to both sides of a trade from the trade date forward. Best Trade is the one that helped both managers the most — the trade maximizing the smaller of the two sides' value received, i.e. nobody left empty-handed. Worst Trade is the most lopsided — the biggest gap between what each side got.
